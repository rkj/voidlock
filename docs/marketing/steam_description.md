# Voidlock — About This Game

**You are not a hero. You are a middle manager.**

Somewhere in a corporate operations center, you sit at a green-phosphor terminal. On the other end of a satellite uplink, your assigned biological assets — marines, technically — are breaching a derelict hull crawling with hostile organisms.

They're trained. They're competent. They explore, open doors, and engage hostiles without your input. But they're not smart enough to know when to fall back, when to change tactics, when to abandon the objective and run.

That's your job.

**VOIDLOCK** is a deterministic real-time tactics game with autonomous squad AI. Issue commands through a menu-driven terminal interface. Change engagement rules. Redirect squads. Trigger extraction before the swarm overruns the corridor. Or don't — replacement assets are cheaper than you'd think.

### Key Features

*   **Autonomous Marines**: Your biological assets are fully competent. They explore, breach, and fight autonomously. Your role is strategic intervention: redirection, engagement policy shifts, and retrieval authorization.
*   **Corporate Remote Interface**: A keyboard-driven menu system. No click-to-move. This is a remote operations feed on corporate-issue hardware, not a video game.
*   **Deterministic Simulation**: Every operation is reproducible. Same seed, same outcome. Replay the tapes to analyze asset loss and improve operational efficiency.
*   **Procedural Derelicts**: Explore unique ship layouts with tight corridors, blind corners, and lethal chokepoints. Every contract is a new architectural puzzle.
*   **Fog of War**: You see only what your assets telemetry allows. Blind corners stay blind until a unit rounds them.
*   **Roguelite Contracts**: Manage a persistent roster, procure advanced loadouts, and meet quarterly targets across multi-mission campaigns.
*   **Operational Budgeting**: Every lost unit is a budget deduction. Every recovered artifact is a bonus. Balance asset risk against corporate rewards.

**Your assets are expendable. Your quarterly review is not.**
